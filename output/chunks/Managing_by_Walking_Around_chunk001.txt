MANAGEMENT BY

WALKING AROUND

(MBWA)

November 2020

What is MBWA?

• MBWA is a management style in which the managers walk around in

unstructured and unplanned manner amongst the team.

• Objective:

o Interact with the team and be aware of their work, ask questions while they are performing

it.

o Gather information, which can be beneficial in making decisions and resolving problems.

• Allows the manager to better understand the issues, ideas, and concerns

of the team.

• Awareness and Involvement.

Key Components

1. Walking Around

• Time to walk around the team.

• Spontaneous and non-planned interactions.

• Getting up and away from the desk.

• Walk amongst the team that you are focused on & within your control.

Do not cover areas that are not subjected to your control.

Nov 2020 4

Key Components

2. Striking Up Conversations

• Don’t walk around aimlessly saying “Hi”.

• Conversations should be directly related to the work the person or the

business is doing, and occasionally touch on private issues.

• Focus should be on learning from these informal situations.

Nov 2020 5

Key Components

3. Creating Trust

• Encourage better relationships and open communication (Trust).

• Even if the discussion does not lead to new information, the interaction

between the manager and the team helps to strengthen communication

and build stronger bonds (Trust).

• When problems do arise, it is easier to share that information with

everyone.

• Sets a better example of communication throughout the organization.

What Good Looks Like

Managers listen to people

• Listen to what the team is telling you.

• Become good at reading the subtle signs people are telling you.

• The team might not feel comfortable being very honest with you, especially right at the

start of implementing the new method (DC example).

Managers use discussion as an opportunity to transmit the company’s

values

• Walks and talks provide you the ability to strengthen the company’s values and vision

(culture).

Managers are willing to provide on-the-spot assistance and support

• If your team requires help, you must be willing to lead by example and provide support

on the spot.

• Demonstrates a sense of urgency, and it isn’t about “looking into it”, but getting problems

fixed quickly.

Benefits of Walking Around

Creating stronger communication channels and improving

relationships

• Build trust and relationships.

• Motivates team by suggesting that management takes an active interest in people.

• Strengthens ability to drive cultural change for higher organizational performance.

• Makes work less formal.

• Creates a healthy organization.

Nov 2020 8

Benefits of Walking Around

Helping everyone focus on the correct operational aspects

• Build trust and relationships.

• Get a first-hand experience of what is really going on.

• Ensures a better understanding of the correct operational decisions that must be made.

• Enhances understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of the organization.

• Strengthens an environment of feedback where ideas, tips, and suggestions are openly shared.

• “Management bubble” is removed; managers don’t see just what they want to see and what the

team wants to present.

• The antidote to “Them vs Us”.

Dangers and things to be cautious of

Time consuming strategy

• Can take a lot of the manger’s time.

• Not every conversation is going to lead to an immediate benefit.

• Insights are random in terms of the immediate benefit.

Emphasizing the importance of employee opinion

• Cautious of too much emphasis or overemphasizing with the team.

• Don’t pay too much attention and lose site of those by the customers or other stakeholders.

• Stay focused on the Customer.
