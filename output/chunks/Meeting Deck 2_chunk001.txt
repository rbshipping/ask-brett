How to Use This Document

The following slides will help you lead your team through the second meeting of the Playing to Win process.

Each slide includes talking points in the notes field. Use these to prepare your presentation and guide your discussion.

Customize the slides as needed. You may want to add your organization’s branding, insert new slides, adjust existing slides, and/or revise the talking points.

Before proceeding, familiarize yourself with the Facilitator’s Guide, Case Studies, and Video Tips (see next slide for more detail).

Meeting Deck 2

[Slide 2]

Additional Materials

In addition to these slides, your Playing to Win Strategy Toolkit includes:

A Facilitator’s Guide that walks you step-by-step through the process, giving you information on how to prepare and run your meetings. It may be helpful to read through the Facilitator’s Guide before customizing the Meeting Decks.

Four Case Studies that illustrate how different companies have worked through the Playing to Win process. Use them to get comfortable with the process and/or share them with your team.

Video Tips for you to view yourself and/or to share with your team. The videos answer some of the most common questions about the process.

OIL OF OLAYGenerate Possibilities

<number>

CHOICE 1:

Invest to reinvent Oil of Olay, turning it into a winning brand.

STRATEGIC PROBLEM:

How to become a global leader in skin care

CHOICE 2:

Shut down Oil of Olay and invest in another skin-care brand.

POSSIBILITY 1: Transform Olay into a Premium Brand

Move Olay upmarket by targeting younger women and offering products that fight earlier and multiple signs of aging.

POSSIBILITY 2: Extend an Existing Brand into Skin Care

Extend one of P&G’s winning brands, like CoverGirl, into skin care.

POSSIBILITY 3: Acquire Another Skin-Care Brand

Buy a leading global skin-care brand and integrate it into the P&G family.
